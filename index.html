The Foreign

Tourist Ecosystem in Malaysia

This visualization provides an interactive overview of Malaysia's
tourism landscape in 2023/2024.

19 September 2025

1
 Where does Malaysia's
Visitors Come From?

Malaysia continues to attract millions of international each year, with neighbouring countries like Singapore, Indonesia and Thailand leading the arrivals. During 2024, arrivals from Singapore surpassed 18 million, reflecting strong cross-border tourism ties and Malaysia's role as a regional hub. While short-haul travellers dominate, long-haul visitors from China, India, and the United Kingdom have also grown sharply, supported by new flight routes and visa-friendly policies.

2
 Air and Land Dominate Foreign
Visitor Arrivals

The pie chart shows foreign tourist arrivals to Malaysia by mode of transport in 2023 and 2024. Air travel remains dominant, carrying nearly 11 million visitors in 2024—a 40.3% increase from the previous year—while land transport accounts for over 25 million arrivals, growing 29.5%. Sea travel grew moderately by 13% to around 1.3 million, whereas rail travel declined slightly by 4.4%, representing just 1.5% of arrivals.

3
 Expenditure Breakdown
of Visitors

Tourist spending reveals a clear hierarchy of priorities. Basic needs like accommodation and dining total over $36,000, while leisure, led by shopping at nearly $40,000, dominates discretionary spending. Travel costs reflect the backbone of journeys, and smaller outlays on medical services, tours, fuel, and other expenses hint at the unpredictable realities of life on the road. The data paints travelers as pragmatic in necessities, eager in pleasures, and mindful of the journey itself.

4
 Where Malaysia Stays:
Hotel Guests and Occupancy

Hotel occupancy across Malaysia paints a varied landscape. Kuala Lumpur leads with strong gains in foreign guests, boosting overall occupancy, while Putrajaya and Pulau Pinang also see significant growth. Domestic demand drives notable increases in Kedah and Johor, though some regions, like Perak, Perlis, and Labuan, experience declines. Overall, the figures reveal a recovering hospitality sector, propelled by international travelers and selective domestic growth, yet uneven across states.

5
 From Exchange Rates to Arrivals:
A 2-Year View

Foreign tourist arrivals in Malaysia proved surprisingly resilient despite a weakening ringgit. Through much of 2023, the USD/MYR rate hovered between 4.5 and 4.8, while arrivals fluctuated, topping 3 million mid-year before dipping early 2024. By August 2024, visitor numbers surged to 4.5 million, suggesting that a softer currency may have boosted Malaysia's appeal. The steady climb in arrivals underscores the country's growing magnetism for travelers and the robustness of its tourism sector.

6
 Tourism Hotspots Across
Malaysia In 2023

The bubble chart shows foreign tourist arrivals across Malaysian states, with darker shades indicating higher numbers. West Malaysia leads, with Johor, Kedah, Kuala Lumpur, and Selangor attracting the most visitors. East Malaysia sees more moderate arrivals, mainly in Sabah and Sarawak from China, Taiwan, South Korea, and Brunei. The map highlights the concentration of tourism in the West and informs regional planning.

7
 Tourist Entries by
Entry Point State and Month

The visualisation pairs a bar chart and a line chart to tell the story of tourist arrivals in Malaysia from January 2020 through October 2024. The bar chart highlights Johor and Selangor as the busiest entry points, far outpacing other states. Meanwhile, the line chart traces arrivals over time, revealing a steep drop at the outset of the period, followed by a steady recovery and eventual increase, capturing the resilience and gradual rebound of Malaysia’s tourism sector.
